## Completed

### ✓ NIST AI RMF 1.0
**File:** `nist-ai-rmf.html` · **Status:** Live

**What I know:**
- The 4 core functions: GOVERN (culture/policy), MAP (context), MEASURE (evidence), MANAGE (action)
- GOVERN is cross-cutting — it runs through all other functions
- Risk = probability × magnitude of harm; harms can land on individuals, groups, orgs, society, environment
- The 7 trustworthiness characteristics: Valid & Reliable (foundation), Safe, Secure & Resilient, Accountable & Transparent (cross-cutting), Explainable & Interpretable, Privacy-Enhanced, Fair with Bias Managed
- Key tradeoffs: interpretability vs accuracy, privacy vs fairness, security vs transparency
- Three bias categories: systemic, computational/statistical, human-cognitive
- Explainability (how) vs Interpretability (why/meaning) vs Transparency (what happened)
- TEVV = Test, Evaluation, Verification & Validation — ongoing, not just at launch
- Residual risk must be documented and disclosed to end users
- Framework is voluntary, sector-agnostic, scales to any org size
- AI actors = everyone with a hand in the lifecycle; risk is shared, not siloed

**Client angles:**
- "Think ISO 27001, but for AI risk"
- "The RMF gives you the receipts to show clients your AI is responsible, not just powerful"
- "Trustworthiness is a competitive advantage — it wins enterprise procurement"
- AI risks are categorically different from traditional software risks (drift, hallucination, bias at scale)
